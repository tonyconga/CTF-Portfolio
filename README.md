# CTF-Portfolio

## About Me
I was first introduced to cybersecurity during my time in the military, where my MOS (Military Occupational Specialty) required performing digital forensics tasks. That experience sparked my interest in the broader field of cybersecurity, particularly within digital forensics. Motivated by this exposure, I pursued further education through the SANS Technology Institute, where I continued to build both theoretical knowledge and practical skills.

Through my academic experience, I was introduced to the National Cyber League (NCL), which provided an opportunity to explore multiple areas within cybersecurity. This hands-on exposure allowed me to develop a deeper understanding of how different domains within the field connect and operate in real-world scenarios.

## CTF Experience
Participating in CTF challenges has allowed me to apply my training in practical, problem-solving environments. These challenges covered a wide range of domains, including network analysis, log analysis, OSINT, cryptography, web application security, and file/binary analysis. Each challenge required a structured approach to identify relevant data, apply the correct tools, and validate findings.

## Highlights
- Applied digital forensics techniques to analyze files, logs, and network data  
- Used tools such as Wireshark, dig, jq, hex editors, and CyberChef to extract and interpret data  
- Solved challenges across multiple domains including OSINT, cryptography, and web security  
- Developed structured workflows to improve efficiency and accuracy  
- Strengthened ability to pivot between different data sources and correlate findings  

## Reflection
Through these CTF experiences, I improved my ability to approach complex problems methodically and work through unfamiliar scenarios with confidence. One of the most valuable lessons was learning to focus on understanding the underlying data rather than relying solely on tools. I also refined my ability to validate findings and ensure accuracy, which is critical in digital forensics and cybersecurity analysis.
